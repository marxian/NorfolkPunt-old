NORFOLK PUNT RULES 2003

The objective of the Norfolk Punt Class is to promote yacht racing on the Norfolk & Suffolk Broads in vessels which derive from the traditional marsh men’s working boats.

  • A Norfolk Punt is defined as a strongly built, light draught, partly decked sailing craft, with pointed stem and stern and a centerboard and a nearly flat bottom. The gunwale plan view being a fair curve, the sheerline flat with very low freeboard.
  • The Norfolk Punt shall be a class restricted by the following regulations:
    New boats are to comply with either Hull Rules A and be regarded as a hardchine or the open class Hull rules B. All boats are to conform to Hull Rules C.

Hull Rules A.

  1. The minimum weight of the hull in dry condition shall be 250lbs to include all fittings that are permanently attached but not including the centerboard and rudder or rig and rigging.
  2. The crop of the deck shall not exceed 150mm. The deck may be cut down at the stern, but the distance between the deck and the bottom of the hull shall not be less than 150mm.
  3. The width of the rubbing strake shall not exceed 38mm.
  4. At the bow, the distance between the baseline and the centre of the chine shall be 370mm with a tolerance of +/- 15mm. (This measurement should be correct if the chine meets the stem at a point 215mm down from deck measured along the stem.).
  5. At the stern, the distance between the baseline and the centre of the chine shall be a minimum of 267mm at the join of the stern post, the chine angle shall be 180 degrees, in other words there shall be no discontinuity between the sides and the bottom of the hull at this point. The width of the bottom of the hull shall be not less than 50mm at the join of the stern post.
  6. For measurement purposes points P and Q are defined as: P is the true bow and is the intersection of the outside line of the sides (excluding the rubbing strake) and the plane of the deck. Point Q is similarly defined at the stern.
  7. The hull shall be measured at seven stations from a base line which shall be determined as follows:-
    1. At the bow 203mm below the keel at a point 457mm aft of a line perpendicular to the building base line passing through the true bow point P.
    2. At the stern 203mm below the keel at a point 152mm forward of a line perpendicular to the building base line passing through the true stern point Q.

Hull Rules B.

  1. The hull shall be symmetrical athwart ships and pointed at both stem and stern. â€˜Pointedâ€™ shall be defined as maximum 80 degrees subtended angle at the bow and maximum 110 degrees subtended angle at the stern.
  2. The overall beam including any rubbing strake of 50mm maximum width from the sheerline shall not exceed 1829mm.
  3. Fore and aft rocker shall not exceed 152mm measured on the centreline at points 457mm from either end of the boat. A line drawn from the base of the stem head to the base of the stern post along the bottom centreline of the boat shall be a fair curve.
  4. Rise of floor at mid length shall not exceed 127mm at points 610mm at 90 degrees from the centreline.
  5. Vertical depth of the hull at mid length cross section shall not exceed 387mm measured from the top of the deck level with the outer skin to outside of the bottom (excluding keel band if fitted).
  6. The foredeck shall extend aft at least 1219mm from the bow. The stern deck shall extend forward at least 610mm from the stern. The side deck width shall be maximum 305mm excluding rubbing strake and minimum 152mm. The cockpit shall be at least 1830mm in length.
  7. The minimum weight of the hull in dry condition shall be 250lbs (113.4kg) to include all fittings that are permanently attached but not including the centreboard and rudder or rig and rigging. Corrector weights may not exceed 10lbs.

Hull Rules C

  1. The overall length shall not exceed 6756 mm and not be less than 5486mm.
  2. It is the owner’s responsibility to ensure that a waterlogged boat will float supporting the crew.
  3. A girder or web shall run along the centreline of the boat (extending vertically from hog to deck) to include the centreboard case and rudder box at least from a point below the forestay or jib tack to the aft side of the rudder box. This girder must be adequately braced from the mast stepping to the fixing point of the shrouds. Lightening holes may be drilled in the girder or web, but must not affect the strength of the structure.
  4. No fitting (excluding the exit of an asymmetric pole or bow fender) shall project beyond the rubbing strake. An asymmetric exit may not protrude beyond the overall length of the hull.
  5. The centreboard shall be fully retractable and removable from its case.
  6. The rudder shall be housed inboard and easily removable from its case from inside the boat.
  7. The keel band shall not project below the angle formed by the skin of the bottom on either side. A protection strip maybe fitted along the centreline and either side of the centreboard case and rudder box if required.
  8. The class will not carry racks as permanent or temporary fixings to the hull.

Rig

  1. The mast, main boom, spinnaker booms and bowsprit may be of any material and length, but at no time shall the bowsprit, including fittings, project more than 2134mm in front of the stem. A rudder gantry shall not be permitted. A wishbone boom shall not be permitted.
  2. Mainsail
    1. The Luff A shall be measured from the uppermost part of the head of the sail to the lowermost edge of the sail.
    2. The Foot B shall be measured from the outer edge of the sail to the inner most edge of the sail along the foot.
      Note: When a sail has a cut away corner or boltrope partially removed the measurement shall be an elongation of the true edges until they cope to a point.
    3. The shortest distance from the half leech point to the line defined as leech length shall not exceed 686mm.
    4. The shortest distance form the three quarter leech point to the line defined as leech length shall not exceed 610mm.
  3. Jib
    1. The Luff C shall be measured along the luff wire to the edges of the cloth at the tack and at the head D is the shortest distance from the edge of the cloth at the clew to the luff when the sail is dry and fully stretched out on a flat floor.
    2. The roach on the jib shall not exceed 101mm.
  4. Sail Area
    The maximum sail area is 16.35 sq.m. measured according to the formula:

    (A*B) / 2 + ( C*D) / 2

    Areas shall be marked in indelible ink in figures of at least 51mm high on the jib tack and main clew at the time of measuring.
  5. Battens
    1. Mainsail : There shall be no restriction in length or number of battens.
    2. Jib : The number of battens shall not exceed 5. They shall be inserted from the leech of the jib.
  6. Sailcloth
    There shall be no restrictions of material for the mainsail or jib.
  7. Spinnaker
    The spinnaker shall be a three-cornered sail constructed of normal woven sailcloth. No headboard, battens or other stiffening device, other than normal woven cloth reinforcing is allowed. A bowsprit shall be retracted within the length of the hull, except while the spinnaker is set, or during hoisting or lowering thereof.

    Only one spinnaker may be carried in the boat in Punt Class races.

    There shall no requirement for sail numbers on a spinnaker if they are fitted they shall be the correct number for the boat.

    Measurement points and definitions are as in ISAF sail definitions section G.
  8. Sail numbers shall be carried on the Mainsail in accordance with the ISAF rules current at the time of measurement.

Name

Boats are named after birds or fish.

Certificates:

No boat shall be allowed to race in the class unless she has a valid Measurement Certificate. This certificate shall be issued by the Owners' Association Secretary on receipt from the Measurer of a completed Measurement Form showing the boat to conform to Class Rules. Areas and serial numbers of sails measured are to be recorded on the Measurement Certificate. Any boat measured before 1997 (excepting no 84) is deemed to have complied with the rules in force at the time she was measured. Any alteration after 1997 shall comply with the rules current at the time of the alteration. Cases of doubt regarding compliance with the Class Rules shall be referred to the Owners' Association or a committee appointed by the Owners' Association for this purpose.
